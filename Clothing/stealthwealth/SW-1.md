Fair — that was too tied to the logo/polo mockup. For actual wardrobe brands that are Thom Browne-adjacent, ultra-luxury, preppy, and sometimes bright, I’d sort them like this:

Best answers

Brand	Why it fits
Ralph Lauren Purple Label	The cleanest answer for ultra-luxury American prep: blazers, polos, rugby/cricket energy, cashmere, tailored trousers, club colors. More classic than Thom Browne, less ironic.
Casablanca	Bright, resort-preppy, tennis-club, country-club, silk-shirt energy. Less stealth, more flashy, but very aligned with colorful luxury prep.
Bode	Artsy, nostalgic, handmade-feeling luxury with collegiate/preppy undertones. Bright, embroidered, unique, and expensive without looking like mall designer.
Drake’s	Very tasteful Ivy/preppy menswear: rugby shirts, OCBDs, chore jackets, ties, blazers, colorful knitwear. Not “ultra luxury” like Loro Piana, but extremely sharp.
Noah	Preppy, nautical, colorful, and smart, though more street/prep than ultra-luxury. Think elevated rugby shirts, polos, striped knits.
Aimé Leon Dore	Modern prep, Greek/New York country-club style, vintage sportswear, loafers, polos, knitwear. More hype-adjacent than stealth, but very wearable.
Brunello Cucinelli	Ultra-luxury quiet wealth. Not as bright, but the quality is dead-on. Best for cream, pale blue, washed pink, taupe, linen, suede, cashmere.
Loro Piana	Peak stealth wealth. Less playful, but unbeatable for luxury polos, knitwear, outerwear, summer resort pieces.
Isaia	Italian luxury with more color and personality: bright blues, coral, yellow, soft tailoring, patterned shirts. More fun than Cucinelli/Loro Piana.
Etro	Bright, patterned, colorful luxury. Less stealth, more peacock-preppy/resort. Good if you want pizazz.
Paul Smith	British tailoring with color, stripes, wit, and preppy polish. Less ultra-luxury than Kiton/Loro Piana, but closer to Thom Browne’s playful menswear spirit.
Kiton	Ultra-expensive Italian tailoring/casualwear. Can be bright in the Naples luxury way. Less collegiate, more yacht/Capri.
Hermès	True ultra-luxury, stealth if you avoid obvious belts/logos. Their bright polos, scarves, knitwear, and leather goods can hit colorful-preppy hard.
Lacoste x higher-end capsules / Lacoste runway	Not ultra-luxury normally, but stylistically important: bright, tennis-preppy, clean, logo heritage.
Rowing Blazers	Very bright and preppy, but not stealth or ultra-luxury. More fun reference brand than “quiet money.”

Thom Browne itself is known for precise construction, uniformity, tailoring, and altered proportion, so brands closest in spirit need some mix of tailoring, prep, and recognizable design language.  ￼ For the quieter luxury side, Loro Piana, Brunello Cucinelli, Zegna, Kiton, and The Row are commonly treated as core understated-luxury names, with Loro Piana especially associated with simple design and exceptional fabrics.  ￼ For the classic preppy side, guides consistently point to Ralph Lauren, Brooks Brothers, J.Crew, Aurélien, Luca Faloni, and similar Ivy/smart-casual brands.  ￼

My actual shortlist for your description: Ralph Lauren Purple Label, Casablanca, Bode, Drake’s, Isaia, Brunello Cucinelli, and Loro Piana.
